Mapping vehicle pollution and vegetation removed pollution in USA

The goal of this project is: Mapping vehicle pollution and vegetation removed pollution in USA. And mapping Canopy Coverage, SO2 pollution and PM2.5 pollution.

The client, Prof Kyuha Lee would like to visually observe current environmental pattern through visualizations, and to explain the causes of current pattern by comparing multiple visualizations. And He want to gain some insights from this visualization that can help protect the environment.

Data description: County boundaries from: https://www.census.gov/geographies/mapping-files/time-series/geo/carto-boundary-file.html. Data of canopy coverage, vehicle pollution and vegetation removed pollution from Argonne National Laboratory Data of PM2.5 concentration from [PM2.5 by County](https://gist.github.com/georgeeeee/0342e8e427e75c369d5981550dd4b83c).

Our observations are: 1. SO2 is the least absorbed pollutant in automobile pollution. The plot of SO2 content corresponds to the plot of plant absorption of pollution. 2.By comparing the vegetation coverage map and PM2.5 map, it is found that the areas with the highest vegetation coverage are also the areas that produce the most pollution. The United States has reached a balance between pollution and treatment.
